how it works

your time becomes the chips.

1

get your daily bankrolls

Every midnight, your wallets refill: Social (split across Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok & YouTube), Entertainment, and Games. That's your stake for the day.

2

bet minutes, not money

Sit at any table and wager time from any wallet — bet your whole Social pool or go surgical and stake just your TikTok minutes.

3

win time. or lose it.

Win → more scrolling time today, a hotter streak (up to +25% payouts), and rank progress. Lose → that wallet shrinks. Hit zero and those apps are done for the day.

4

broke? there's a bank.

One loan per wallet per day: get time now, but tomorrow starts short. Borrowing costs you — that's the lesson, gamified.

the fine print, up front

not gambling. anti-gambling.

  • No real money — ever. Nothing can be deposited, bought, won, sold or cashed out. There are no prizes and no in-app purchases. The stakes are minutes of your own screen-time goals.

questions people actually ask

faq

wait — is this gambling?
No, and it's designed to be the opposite. Gambling requires real money and real prizes — Screen Stakes has neither, by hard rule. You bet minutes of your own daily screen-time budget against yourself. It borrows the psychology of the casino (stakes, streaks, the sweat) and points it at using your phone less.
does it actually block my apps?
The current version is an honor system — the wallets track your budget but don't physically lock anything yet. Real blocking through Apple's official Screen Time framework is the next major release: apps genuinely lock at 0:00, and winning at the tables unlocks them.
